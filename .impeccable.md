# Papr — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Broad general desktop users who want a native, local-first RSS reader. Reading
habits are mixed — some track many feeds and skim, others read a few feeds
deeply. The app must serve fast triage *and* unhurried reading without forcing
either mode. Used on macOS/Windows/Linux desktops, typically in a focused
session (morning catch-up, a break, end of day).

### Brand Personality
Calm, literate, unhurried. Three words: **quiet, editorial, crafted.** The
interface should feel like a well-set page — confident typography, generous
margins, nothing shouting. It evokes the trust of print: the content is the
star, the chrome recedes. Never trendy, never loud, never "appy."

### Aesthetic Direction
"Editorial Quiet." Warm paper-toned surfaces (not white, not gray), a single
terracotta-clay accent used sparingly, a Newsreader serif for reading and
brand moments paired with Inter Tight for UI and JetBrains Mono for metadata.
Both light and dark themes ship; dark is a warm low-light paper, not a cold
black. Three-pane desktop layout (sidebar / list / reader).

Anti-references: SaaS dashboards, neon-on-black "AI" aesthetics, glassmorphism,
gradient accents, card-grid monotony, purple/blue tech gradients.

### Design Principles
1. **The page is the product.** Reading surfaces get the most care; chrome
   stays quiet and recedes when not in use.
2. **One accent, used rarely.** Terracotta marks the single most important
   thing on screen — never decoration.
3. **Warmth in every neutral.** No pure black or white; every surface and
   hairline is tinted toward the paper hue.
4. **Typographic hierarchy over boxes.** Lead with type weight, size, and
   space — not borders, cards, or fills.
5. **Density is a choice, not a default.** Compact/cozy/spacious must each feel
   deliberately set, not just scaled.
